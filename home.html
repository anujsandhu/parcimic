Emergency Response Protocol

Advanced emergency protocols for life-threatening situations in India, including when to call 112/108, emergency services coordination, and critical care guidelines.

Emergency Protocol Steps:

  • Immediate Assessment: Check responsiveness, assess breathing, identify life threats
  • Emergency Call (112): Provide exact location, describe emergency, follow dispatcher guidance
  • Critical Interventions: CPR if trained, bleeding control, safe positioning
  • Ambulance Protocols: Clear access path, gather medical info, prepare for transport
  • Hospital Coordination: Contact nearest facility, provide patient details, arrange reception

Comprehensive First Aid

Professional first aid techniques for common injuries, burns, fractures, snake bites, and medical emergencies specific to Indian geographical and climatic conditions.

First Aid Protocols:

  • Bleeding Control: Direct pressure with sterile dressing, elevate injured area, pressure points if needed
  • Burn Management: 20 minutes cool running water, remove non-sticking clothing, sterile non-adherent dressing
  • Snake Bite Protocol: Immobilize affected limb, keep below heart level, transport to hospital
  • Fracture Care: Immobilize with splints, avoid moving patient, seek medical help
  • Choking Response: Back blows, abdominal thrusts, CPR if unconscious

Medication Management

Complete medication guide for Indian pharmaceutical products, dosage guidelines, side effects, interactions, and consultation protocols with healthcare providers.

Medication Safety Practices:

  • Safe Medication Practices: Verify with registered pharmacist, check generic vs brand names, confirm manufacturing date
  • Storage Conditions: Validate temperature requirements, protect from moisture, keep away from children
  • Critical Precautions: Complete prescribed course, time doses accurately, monitor for adverse effects
  • Allergy Management: Report allergies immediately, carry epinephrine if prescribed, wear medical alert jewelry
  • Drug Interactions: Inform all doctors of current medications, avoid alcohol interactions, watch for side effects
